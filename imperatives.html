Examples of naturally occurring imperatives

What follows is a list consisting of examples of various kinds of naturally occurring imperatives. Most of the examples have been taken from the British National Corpus (BNC).

  1. Simple clause
    • Tell me more. (BNC; C85 2958)
    • Buy us drinks. (BNC; F9M 1383)
    • 'Swim to the shore!' (BNC; FRX 72)
  2. Negation
    • Just don't tell me then. (BNC; KBF 2378)
    • And don't be beastly to him. (BNC; ACE 2769)
    • Don't stop or pick up another fare for at least another five blocks after you drop me. (BNC; CDA 952)
  3. Conjunction
    • Come right in and close the door. (BNC; CK9 1376)
    • Take a look at yourself in a mirror and judge for yourself if you measure up. (BNC; BN9 989)
    • Choose one of the shops and list the questions you would ask if you wanted to find out whether it was needed in your area. (BNC; B2U 2249)
    • Refer to the theatre page and choose the area in which you wish to sit and the price you wish to pay per seat. (BNC; B3K 270)
    • Be extra vigilant about what you eat or drink and with what or whom you come into contact. (BNC; BNL 2001)
  4. Disjunction
    • Either leave or wallop them; one or the other. (BNC; CDT 65)
    • No one is to leave or enter without my permission. (BNC; H98 3479)
    • Paint them deep matt, eggshell or shiny gloss and hang them with pictures or a collection of some sort. (BNC; HGW 1612)
    • Remove the inhaler from your mouth and hold your breath for about 10 seconds, or as long as is comfortable then breathe out slowly. (PIL; Aerocrom Inhaler.sgml)
  5. Pseudo-imperatives
    • Sack this tadger immediately --; or I'll be cancelling my order for Sounds. (BNC; HWX 2591 )
    • Let me get in the queue look or we'll miss our turn. (BNC; KB8 11404)
    • Stop it now stop or she'll get burnt won't she? (BNC; KD4 44)
    • Answer up, or I'll tread on your foot. (BNC; CKE 1625)
    • Give the door a firm push and you are certain it's well and truly shut. (BNC; CFR 909)
  6. Conditional imperatives
    • 6.2 Tell the OU Computing Helpdesk if you think that someone else knows your password. (OU Terms and Conditions for Students)
    • If you do not want to become a member, please write to OUSA at PO Box 397, Walton Hall, Milton Keynes MK7 6BE. (OU Terms and Conditions for Students)
  7. Deontic/Modal
    • 50.Your application should fulfil the criteria below, or will be considered ineligible for support: (page 15; AHRC Guidelines)
    • You should also provide a curriculum vitae of the proposed researcher if one is named, otherwise you should state the skills and qualifications sought. (page 56;AHRC Guidelines)
    • Also, if you are drinking alcohol you should stop or at least cut down on low alcohol drinks or extending alcoholic drinks with low calorie mixers can be helpful. (BNC; AR7 647)
    • You have to let go or go mad. (BNC; A0L 3343)
    • I must go or I'll miss my train. (BNC; CEY 1407)
    • You should not drink tea, cola, coffee or cocoa. (PIL; Adenoscan.sgml)

Paul Piwek I am a research fellow at the ITRI  in Brighton. My main areas of interest are formal and computational models of dialogue and discourse semantics. I have been working mainly within the frameworks of Discourse Representation Theory and Constructive Type Theory (roughly speaking, also known as Pure Type Systems or Typed Lambda Calculus).

In addition to my work as a researcher, I am responsible for the library and the  library webpages (internal access only) of the  ITRI

Background 
 

 

 I received (the Dutch equivalent of) an MA in Computational Linguistics from Tilburg University in 1993 (supervisor: Dr. R. Muskens) and a postgraduate degree in the Philosophy of Linguistics and Cognitive Science from the University of Amsterdam in 1994 (supervisor: Prof. dr. R. Bartsch). During my studies I did a traineeship at the IMS (University of Stuttgart) (supervisors: Dr. T.E. Zimmermann and Prof. dr. H. Kamp). From May 1994 until May 1998 I was a research assistant at  the IPO in Eindhoven. In that period I wrote my PhD dissertation (see below) and was working on the DENK project. I joined the ITRI  on the 1st of May 1998. At the ITRI I have been working on the CLIME  project.
 
Dissertation I received my PhD from Eindhoven University of Technology in 1998 on a dissertation titled Logic, Information and Conversation. [dissertation]
 
Teaching Together with Emiel Krahmer (IPO, Eindhoven), I teach a course during the 12th European Summer School in Logic, Language and Information (ESSLLI) (6 - 18 August, 2000, Birmingham) titled Varieties of Anaphora. [course]
